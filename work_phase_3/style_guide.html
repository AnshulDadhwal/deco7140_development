Style Guide

A comprehensive overview of the visual design elements, typography, colors, and components used throughout the project. This style guide presents our design proposal for CCI, demonstrating how our design decisions align with user needs and accessibility requirements identified in our research.

Design Levels

Our design approach follows Don Norman's three levels of design: Visceral, Behavioural, and Reflective. Each level addresses different aspects of user experience and emotional engagement.

Visceral Design

Explore the immediate visual impact through layouts, typography, and color schemes. These elements create the first impression and emotional response.

Includes: Page layouts, color palette, typography system

Learn more

Behavioural Design

Discover the interactive elements and usability patterns that guide user behavior. These components ensure intuitive and efficient user interactions.

Includes: Buttons, forms, navigation, micro-interactions

Learn more

Reflective Design

Understand how design creates meaning and builds lasting relationships. These choices reflect brand values and create emotional connections.

Includes: Brand messaging, storytelling, emotional resonance

Design Philosophy

Our design proposal is grounded in the user research and client needs identified in our Design Research phase. Every design decision can be traced back to specific user stories, accessibility requirements, and business objectives.

Key principles guiding our design:

  • Accessibility First: All designs meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards minimum
  • Mobile-First Approach: Optimized for mobile devices with progressive enhancement for desktop
  • User-Centered: Based on real user needs and behaviors documented in our research
  • Brand Alignment: Reflects CCI's values and business objectives
