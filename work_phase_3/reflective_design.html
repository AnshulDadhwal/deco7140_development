Meaning through Imagery and Tone

Analysing an existing site

I looked at the UQ Alumni Network website because it serves a similar audience to Global Community Connect. The first impression it gives is warm and professional. The images show real people at events, smiling and interacting, which helps create a sense of connection and achievement. The site values belonging, pride, and community. Its tone is friendly but still formal enough to feel credible. The visuals and words work together quite well, though some of the imagery feels very academic and less focused on cultural diversity. It includes alumni and professionals easily but might overlook students who are new or still settling in. It meets most client needs but could do more to highlight personal stories and cross cultural connection.

Identity and emotional tone

Global Community Connect should project belonging, inclusivity, optimism, and empowerment. The emotional tone should feel warm and hopeful. Visitors should feel welcome, comfortable to share their stories, and confident that they are part of something bigger.

Language and Expression

Reviewing other sites

I looked at the UQ Alumni Network website again. Its tone feels friendly and professional, but it can sound formal and less personal for new international users. The language works well for alumni but does not always show the openness or diversity that a global audience would expect.

Inclusive language checklist

I will use people-first and gender-neutral language, avoid stereotypes, write in clear English, and refer to groups respectfully. These choices make the site easy to read and help everyone feel valued and included.

Accessibility Audit

This audit used the IBM Equal Access checker on behavioural_design.html. Below are three issues I will address in the final build.

1) Text contrast is too low

WCAG: 1.4.3 Contrast (Minimum) AA

Where: Header links such as <a href="index.html"> and <a href="style_guide.html">, and the primary button.

Why it matters: Low contrast makes links and buttons hard to read for many users, especially on small screens or in bright light.

Fix: Increase the color contrast of link and button text against their backgrounds so it meets AA. I will adjust according to the colour wheel to ensure sufficient contrast.

2) Focus indicator is not clearly visible

WCAG: 2.4.7 Focus Visible AA

Where: Navigation links like <a href="index.html">, <a href="site_map.html">, and the current page link.

Why it matters: Keyboard users must see where focus is to move through the site with confidence.

Fix: Add a clear focus style on interactive elements. I will use a clear outline on focus for links and buttons. I will also test with only the keyboard.

3) Form fields need grouping and clear relationships

WCAG: 1.3.1 Info and Relationships A

Where: Related inputs like text fields, textareas, and the file upload.

Why it matters: Screen reader users rely on structure to understand which fields belong together and what they do.

Fix: Wrap related inputs in a <fieldset>.
