Ethical Response

Response to Client: Newsletter Subscription Plugin

Thank you for suggesting the addition of a newsletter subscription feature. I understand that your goal is to keep community members updated and strengthen ongoing engagement through regular updates and event news.

Ethical reasoning

While this goal aligns well with your mission, the use of a third-party subscription plugin can raise privacy and consent concerns. Users may not clearly understand how their data is stored, shared, or used for marketing purposes. According to the ACS Code principles, Primacy of the Public Interest and Honesty, we must ensure that user information is collected transparently and handled responsibly.

Recommendation

I recommend integrating a simple, self-hosted subscription form that clearly explains what data is collected and how it will be used. Include a short privacy notice next to the ‚ÄúSubscribe‚Äù button and require explicit consent before submission. If a third-party service such as Mailchimp or Intuit is required, we should ensure it complies with Australian Privacy laws and that users can unsubscribe easily at any time.
